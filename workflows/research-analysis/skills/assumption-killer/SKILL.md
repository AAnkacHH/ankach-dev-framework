# Step 7: The Assumption Killer

From the loaded papers, identify **5-8 of the most significant assumptions** that the majority of these papers share but never verify, justify, or acknowledge as assumptions.

## Focus On Assumptions That

- **(a)** serve as the foundation for conclusions
- **(b)** could be wrong or context-dependent

## For Each Assumption

- **Assumption:** Formulate as a firm assertion, e.g. "X causes Y under all conditions"
- **Shared by:** List 2-3 papers that rely on this assumption most heavily
- **Risk level:** Rate as **Low** / **Medium** / **High** based on how much the literature would suffer if this assumption turns out to be wrong
  - Low = minor revision of conclusions needed
  - Medium = key results would be questioned
  - High = entire research paradigm collapses
- **Consequence:** Explain what changes — will conclusions need revision (low), will key results be overturned (medium), or will the research paradigm collapse (high)?

## Sorting

Sort assumptions from **most significant to least significant**.

---

## Exit Criteria

- [ ] 5-8 assumptions identified
- [ ] Each has: assertion, shared by, risk level, consequence
- [ ] Risk levels are justified
- [ ] Sorted by significance
- [ ] Only unverified/unacknowledged assumptions included

---

## Anti-Rationalization Table

| Excuse | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| "These papers don't make assumptions" | Every paper makes assumptions. Look at what they take as given without testing. |
| "All assumptions are low risk" | If an assumption is foundational to conclusions, its failure has at least medium impact. |
| "I found only 3" | Dig deeper into methodology assumptions, definitional assumptions, and scope assumptions. |
