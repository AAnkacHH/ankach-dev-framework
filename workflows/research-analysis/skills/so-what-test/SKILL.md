# Step 9: The "So What?" Test

Summarize the entire body of research for a smart person without expert knowledge who has never read any of it. The answer must be exactly **3 numbered points**, each **2-3 sentences maximum**.

Write as if explaining to a smart non-expert — no jargon, no hedging.

## Output Format

**1. What has been proven:** The single strongest and most reliable conclusion from this literature. Formulate as a direct assertion — no "suggests", "might indicate", or "may point to".

**2. What remains unknown:** The most important thing this field has been unable to explain. Be honest, without trying to smooth over the uncertainty.

**3. Why it matters:** The single most important practical implication in the real world. If there is no direct application, name the main theoretical consequence.

<RULES>
- No complex jargon
- No citations or references to sources
- No hedging language or weasel phrases
- If you are not confident enough to make a claim based on these papers, say so directly — do not fabricate confidence
</RULES>

---

## Exit Criteria

- [ ] Exactly 3 points
- [ ] Each point is 2-3 sentences max
- [ ] No jargon, no citations, no hedging
- [ ] Understandable by a non-expert
- [ ] Based solely on the loaded papers

---

## Anti-Rationalization Table

| Excuse | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| "I need more points to be complete" | Exactly 3. Constraint forces prioritization. |
| "I should add caveats" | No. Direct assertions or honest "we don't know". No middle ground. |
| "The research is too nuanced for simple statements" | If you can't explain it simply, you haven't understood it deeply enough. |
