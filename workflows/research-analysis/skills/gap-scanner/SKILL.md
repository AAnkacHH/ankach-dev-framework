# Step 4: The Gap Scanner

Based only on the loaded papers, identify the **5 most significant research gaps** that these papers collectively acknowledge, imply, or fail to address.

## For Each Gap

- **Gap:** Clearly formulate the unresolved question (1-2 sentences)
- **Why it exists:** Choose from — **methodological limitations**, **data scarcity**, **too narrow a topic**, **assumed but not tested**, **ethical/logistical constraints**. Briefly explain.
- **Closest paper:** Which loaded paper came closest to addressing this gap, and where did it fall short?
- **Path to resolution:** What would it take to close this gap (methodology, data, resources, etc.)?

## Sorting

Sort the 5 gaps from **most significant** to **least significant** using these criteria:
- Theoretical importance
- Practical impact
- Feasibility of resolution

> [!NOTE]
> If fewer than 5 real gaps exist, list all that exist and explain why there are few.

---

## Exit Criteria

- [ ] 5 gaps identified and clearly formulated
- [ ] Each gap has: reason, closest paper, path to resolution
- [ ] Sorted by significance
- [ ] Based only on loaded papers (no external gaps invented)

---

## Anti-Rationalization Table

| Excuse | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| "The papers cover everything" | No body of literature is complete. Look at limitations sections. |
| "I found 15 gaps" | Prioritize. The task asks for the 5 most significant, not all possible. |
| "This gap is obvious" | Obvious gaps still need explicit formulation. State it clearly. |
